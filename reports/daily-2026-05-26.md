**National Update - Why students need to matter more than institutions**
*Published: 2026-05-25 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

ITECA's latest national update argues that government policy is restricting choices for students and businesses in tertiary education. The piece takes the position that institutional interests are being prioritised over student outcomes, at a time when the newly-established Australian Tertiary Education Commission (ATEC) is consolidating oversight of both VET and higher education. RTOs operating across both sectors should watch how this centralisation debate shapes future funding and enrolment policy.

**Government sidelines regulator using own bent regulatory stick**
*Published: 2026-05-18 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

ITECA reports that the Australian Government moved to restrict innovation in international education by circumventing the regulator. The piece is critical of the government applying direct regulatory pressure outside formal channels (TEQSA/ASQA). RTOs with CRICOS registration should watch this — if the government is making decisions on international education outside the standard regulatory framework, it affects compliance planning and enrolment forecasting for CRICOS providers.
