**National Update - Why students need to matter more than institutions**
*Published: 2026-05-25 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

ITECA's latest national update argues that government policy is restricting choices for students and businesses in tertiary education. **Government sidelines regulator using own bent regulatory stick**
*Published: 2026-05-18 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

ITECA reports that the Australian Government has moved to restrict innovation in international education by circumventing the regulator. The piece is critical of the government applying direct regulatory pressure rather than working through formal channels (TEQSA/ASQA). RTOs with CRICOS registration should watch this closely — if the government is making decisions about international education outside the standard regulatory framework, it affects compliance planning and enrolment forecasting for CRICOS providers.

Angle for LinkedIn: The government is making calls on international education without going through the regulator — if you're a CRICOS provider, that matters more than you might think.
