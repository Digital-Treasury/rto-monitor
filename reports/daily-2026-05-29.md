### ITECA News

**ITECA welcomes appointment of ATEC Commissioners**
*Published: 2026-05-28 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

ITECA has welcomed the appointment of Professor Barney Glover AO as the inaugural Chief Commissioner of the Australian Tertiary Education Commission (ATEC). The ATEC is the new federal body consolidating oversight of VET and higher education under one framework. Glover steps into the role from his position as Commissioner of Jobs and Skills Australia. For RTOs, particularly dual-sector providers, this appointment signals the ATEC is now operationally active — regulatory and funding policy that previously sat across separate agencies will increasingly route through ATEC.

### Jobs and Skills Australia

**Professor Barney Glover departs Jobs and Skills Australia**
*Published: 2026-05-28 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

JSA's Commissioner Professor Barney Glover has departed to take up the inaugural Chief Commissioner role at ATEC (see ITECA item above). JSA shapes which qualifications get workforce planning priority and feeds into funding decisions across the VET sector. ### NCVER

**New NCVER research highlights how culturally responsive learning can improve outcomes for Aboriginal learners**
*Published: 2026-05-28 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

NCVER released a new report examining place-based and culturally responsive VET for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander learners, with a companion webinar scheduled. The research covers how grounding training in local cultural context improves completion and employment outcomes. RTOs delivering to Aboriginal communities — particularly in remote or regional areas — should review the findings against their current training design and support practices, as this kind of research often feeds into updated guidance under the Standards for RTOs.
