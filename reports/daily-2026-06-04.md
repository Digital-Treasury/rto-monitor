## New items

### TEQSA News

**Education agents and commissions**
*Published: 2026-06-04 | Relevance: HIGH*

TEQSA has reminded all ESOS Act providers — including CRICOS-registered RTOs — that they are now banned from paying education agents a commission for recruiting overseas students who transfer from another provider. The ban took effect 1 April 2026 under amendments to the ESOS Act and the National Code, and also introduces broader definitions of "education agent" and "commission." RTOs must ensure PRISMS records are accurate, agent management processes are updated, staff guidance is revised, and record-keeping is sufficient to demonstrate compliance.

Content-worthy: YES
LinkedIn angle: The April 1 ESOS change banning agent commissions for onshore student transfers has been live for two months — TEQSA's reminder today suggests many providers haven't acted. If you recruit international students, check your agent contracts and PRISMS records now.

URL: https://www.teqsa.gov.au/about-us/news-and-events/latest-news/education-agents-and-commissions

### ITECA News

**ITECA – English Australia MOU Announced; International Education Benefits**
*Published: 2026-06-01 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

ITECA and English Australia have signed a Memorandum of Understanding to deepen collaboration between the two peak bodies on international education policy. The agreement is aimed at better representing providers across both ELICOS and broader independent tertiary education sectors. No direct compliance obligations flow from this MOU, but it signals closer coordination on advocacy affecting international student recruitment policy.
