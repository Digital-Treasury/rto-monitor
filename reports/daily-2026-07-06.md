### ASIC Newsroom

**ASIC issues update on compliance with the financial adviser qualifications standard**
*Published: 2026-07-02 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

ASIC completed a review of the Financial Advisers Register and found 132 existing advisers with no qualifications marked toward the standard that took effect 1 January 2026. Following regulator intervention with 82 AFS licensees, 106 records were updated — but 26 advisers had their authorisation to give personal advice to retail clients ceased. RTOs delivering financial services (FNS) qualifications should ensure graduates understand their obligation to have their qualifications properly recorded on the FAR, and advise AFS licensees accordingly.

Content-worthy: YES
LinkedIn angle: 26 financial advisers just lost their right to give personal advice — not for misconduct, but because their qualifications weren't recorded on the register. A wake-up call for financial services RTOs and their graduates.

### Jobs and Skills Australia

**New data maps how Australians move between jobs**
*Published: 2026-07-02 | Relevance: MEDIUM*

JSA released an Occupation Mobility report and interactive tool showing how workers transition between roles. Network analysis groups occupations into eight mobility clusters (e.g. Social, Education and Service; Engineering and ICT) and identifies "gateway" occupations like Sales Assistant that connect to a wide range of other roles. For RTOs, the tool helps position qualifications in terms of the career pathways they open — and may flag which credential clusters attract more student demand over time.
