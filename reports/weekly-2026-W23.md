## Lead items

### 1. ESOS Act — agent commission ban now in force (HIGH)
**TEQSA News | 2026-06-04**

TEQSA issued a compliance reminder that ESOS Act providers — including CRICOS-registered RTOs — are now banned from paying education agents a commission for recruiting overseas students transferring from another provider. The change took effect 1 April 2026 under amendments to the ESOS Act and National Code. It also broadens the definitions of "education agent" and "commission." RTOs must update agent contracts, PRISMS records, staff guidance, and record-keeping to demonstrate compliance.

URL: https://www.teqsa.gov.au/about-us/news-and-events/latest-news/education-agents-and-commissions

### 2. ATEC now operational — inaugural Chief Commissioner appointed (MEDIUM)
**ITECA News | 2026-05-28 | JSA | 2026-05-28**

The Australian Tertiary Education Commission (ATEC) appointed Professor Barney Glover AO as its inaugural Chief Commissioner. Glover stepped across from Jobs and Skills Australia (JSA), which will now operate under new leadership. ATEC consolidates oversight of VET and higher education under one framework and is now operationally active. Regulatory guidance and funding policy previously spread across multiple agencies will increasingly route through ATEC. Dual-sector RTOs in particular should monitor how ATEC shapes regulatory and funding decisions over the coming months.

### 3. Regional hiring difficulties deepening (MEDIUM)
**Jobs and Skills Australia | 2026-06-03 & 2026-06-04**

JSA's March 2026 Occupation Shortage Report and Regional Labour Market Indicators both confirm that regional employers are experiencing significantly greater hiring challenges than metropolitan counterparts, with major city labour markets outperforming regional areas. Shortages are most acute in trades, health, and care sectors. RTOs delivering in regional markets have a data-backed case for engaging local employers and governments on training investment.

URLs:
- https://www.jobsandskills.gov.au/news/hiring-difficulties-deepen-regional-australia
- https://www.jobsandskills.gov.au/news/major-cities-lead-labour-market-performance-regional-results-remain-mixed

### 4. NCVER research — culturally responsive VET for Aboriginal learners (MEDIUM)
**NCVER | 2026-05-28**

NCVER published a report on place-based and culturally responsive VET for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander learners, with findings on how grounding training in local cultural context improves completion and employment outcomes. A companion webinar was announced. RTOs delivering to Aboriginal communities — particularly in remote or regional areas — should review findings against current training design, as this research type often feeds into updated Standards guidance.

URL: https://www.ncver.edu.au/news-and-events/media-releases/new-ncver-research-highlights-how-culturally-responsive-learning-can-improve-outcomes-for-aboriginal-learners
