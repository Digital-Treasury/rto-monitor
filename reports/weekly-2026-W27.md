## Lead items

### 1. Visa application charges changed on 1 July — CRICOS providers must update student documentation

Visa application charges (VACs) for all Australian visas, including Subclass 500 Student visas, changed effective 1 July 2026 without prior consultation with the education sector. CRICOS-registered RTOs are required to provide accurate cost information to prospective and enrolled international students. Any pre-existing cost guides, enrolment packs, website content, or marketing materials quoting visa fees are now outdated and must be updated before new enrolments are processed. Verify current charges at the Home Affairs fees and charges page.

*Source: ITECA News | 2026-07-01 | Relevance: HIGH*
URL: https://www.iteca.edu.au/web/news/Articles/Government%20Delivers%20Unwelcome%20Visa%20Fee%20Surprise.aspx

### 2. ASQA's new fees take effect — CRIS 2026–27 published

ASQA published its Cost Recovery Implementation Statement (CRIS) for 2026–27 on 29 June. The document details all fees and charges applying to ASQA regulatory activities from 1 July 2026. Any registration, renewal, scope change, or audit activity triggered on or after 1 July is subject to the updated schedule. RTOs with pending regulatory interactions should check the CRIS before initiating any ASQA contact.

*Source: ASQA Latest News | 2026-06-29 | Relevance: HIGH*
URL: https://www.asqa.gov.au/node/704

### 3. ASQA revised how it monitors RTOs — updated audit approach released

ASQA published its revised Regulatory Assessment and Monitoring Approach on 26 June. This document explains how the regulator scopes audits, weights risk, and conducts ongoing monitoring. It is the clearest public signal of what ASQA is looking for and how it prioritises regulatory attention. All RTOs — particularly those with upcoming renewals or compliance history — should read the revised approach.

*Source: ASQA Latest News | 2026-06-26 | Relevance: HIGH*
URL: https://www.asqa.gov.au/node/702

## Other items

### VET outcomes and labour market

**Partial VET completion delivers jobs and pay boost** (JSA, 2026-06-26 | MEDIUM)
JSA research finds that partial VET completions — students who exit before finishing a full qualification — still produce measurable employment and earnings gains. The findings challenge a common assumption that early withdrawal equals failure. Relevant for RTOs managing withdrawal data, communicating outcomes to students, and framing their completion rates in context.
URL: https://www.jobsandskills.gov.au/news/partial-vet-completion-delivers-jobs-and-pay-boost

**Australian Jobs Report 2026: Exploring pathways in a changing world of work** (JSA, 2026-06-30 | MEDIUM)
JSA released the 2026 edition of Australian Jobs — its annual labour market reference covering where employment opportunities exist, which skills are in demand, and how generative AI is reshaping occupations. A useful planning reference for RTOs reviewing their course mix or advising students on career pathways.
URL: https://www.jobsandskills.gov.au/news/australian-jobs-report-2026-exploring-pathways-changing-world-work

### Assessment design

**New resource for gen AI and assurances of learning** (TEQSA, 2026-06-24 | MEDIUM)
TEQSA published the third resource in its assessment reform series, focused on assuring learning quality when gen AI is embedded in student work. The resource highlights evaluative judgement, critical thinking, and ethical reasoning as both quality assurance mechanisms and workforce-readiness outcomes. Relevant to dual-sector RTOs and any provider revising assessment strategy in response to AI tool use.
URL: https://www.teqsa.gov.au/about-us/news-and-events/latest-news/new-resource-gen-ai-and-assurances-learning
