## Lead items

**1. Joint sector alert: ASQA and TEQSA target overseas student transfer practices (HIGH)**
ASQA and TEQSA jointly issued a compliance alert flagging concerns about how some providers advertise to overseas students seeking to transfer. The specific focus is on arrangements that may be inconsistent with the ban on education agent commissions for facilitating transfers. Both regulators expect providers to review their transfer advertising and agent arrangements immediately; non-compliant providers face compliance assessment or regulatory action. This is the most operationally urgent item of the week for RTOs with international students.
Source: TEQSA News | URL: https://www.teqsa.gov.au/about-us/news-and-events/latest-news/joint-sector-alert-issued-overseas-student-transfers

**2. Managed growth arrangements for international students now released (HIGH)**
The Australian Government has published the managed growth allocations that cap international student enrolments at each provider. CRICOS-registered RTOs must check their specific allocation before the next intake cycle — providers who don't check before accepting enrolments risk breaching their allocation. ITECA is also directing members to webinars on agent commission disclosure requirements under recent legislative changes.
Source: ITECA News | URL: https://www.iteca.edu.au/web/news/Articles/International%20Education%20%E2%80%93%20Managed%20growth%20arrangements%20released%2C%20Webinars%20to%20explain%20Agent%20Commission.aspx

**3. Queensland government funding available for TAE training (HIGH)**
QLD government has made funding available for the Certificate IV in Training and Assessment (TAE40122 or equivalent) — the base qualification most RTO trainers must hold. This is a time-limited opportunity for RTOs with Queensland-based trainers who need their TAE or want to upgrade. The ITECA Queensland Update confirms the funding is available now.
Source: ITECA News | URL: https://www.iteca.edu.au/web/news/Articles/ITECA%20Queensland%20Update%20-%20July%202026.aspx

**4. WA Training Programs procurement open (HIGH)**
The WA Training Programs procurement process has opened. RTOs registered in Western Australia can apply to deliver government-funded training under this round. RTOs not already on the WA government panel should check eligibility and submission deadlines urgently.
Source: ITECA News | URL: https://www.iteca.edu.au/web/news/Articles/ITECA%20Western%20Australia%20Update%20-%20July%202026.aspx

**5. Higher Education Threshold Standards amended on racism and governance (HIGH)**
The Australian Government has made amendments to the Higher Education Standards Framework (Threshold Standards) 2021, covering racism and governance obligations. This applies to dual-sector providers — RTOs that are also TEQSA-registered higher education providers. The instrument is on the Federal Register with specific effective dates; TEQSA has flagged sector support materials will follow.
Source: TEQSA News | URL: https://www.teqsa.gov.au/about-us/news-and-events/latest-news/amendments-threshold-standards

## This week's content angles

- ASQA and TEQSA are jointly watching how RTOs advertise to overseas students wanting to transfer — dual-regulator alerts are rare and carry weight. What to check about your agent arrangements and transfer marketing now.
- Managed growth allocations for international students are live — CRICOS RTOs who miss their cap will be unable to enrol more international students until the next cycle. Check yours before your next intake.
- QLD is funding TAE right now. If your trainers still need their Cert IV in Training and Assessment, someone else is about to pay for it — frame as a time-limited opportunity.
- WA Training Programs procurement is open. WA RTOs not on the government funding panel: check eligibility and get your submission in before the window closes.
- Government-funded enrolments are falling across QLD, VIC, SA, and WA simultaneously. This is a national story, not a state one — and RTOs whose revenue is weighted toward government-funded students need a plan.
- The NCVER data on government-funded students through December 2025 is the best current benchmark for where the money is — and isn't — going in VET.
- RTO owners who stay silent during policy consultations don't get to be surprised by the outcomes. The ITECA piece this week is a useful provocation for a LinkedIn post aimed at owners who feel "the system doesn't listen."
